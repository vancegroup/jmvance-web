The VR Factory: Discrete Event Simulation Implemented in a Virtual Environment

Jason Kelsick
Judy M. Vance
Iowa Center for Emerging Manufacturing Technology
Iowa State University
Ames, IA 50011

1998 ASME Design for Manufacture Conference Proceedings, Atlanta, GA, September 13-16, 1998, DETC/CIE 5747.

Abstract
This paper presents an approach to implement virtual reality (VR) as a visualization tool for the discrete event simulation of manufacturing processes. Visualization is easily obtained through VR because it can provide an immersive, interactive three-dimensional environment containing models of all the necessary tools and machines involved in the manufacturing process. The program described in this paper, the VR Factory, integrates results from a discrete event simulation into a virtual, three-dimensional, immersive factory model. The research focuses not only on the creation of a VR replica of a manufacturing process, but also on the animation of the environment and implementation of the results of the discrete event simulation.
