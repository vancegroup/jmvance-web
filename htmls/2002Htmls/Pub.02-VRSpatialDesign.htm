Abstract
Spatial 4C mechanisms are two degree-of-freedom kinematic closed-chains consisting of four rigid links connected by cylindrical, C, joints, which provide both translational and rotational movement. Because of the three-dimensional nature of the spatial mechanism design process, virtual reality is being explored as an improved design interface compared to traditional human-computer interfaces (HCI). Virtual reality provides a more intuitive way to interact with 3D computer models and environments through the use of position trackers and stereo viewing. NSF grants DMI-9872604 and DMI-9816611 have provided support for the development of a virtual environment for spatial mechanism design. The current state of the project is presented in this paper.
