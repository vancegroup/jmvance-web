Virtual Reality as a Human-Computer Interface for Engineering Product Development

Judy M. Vance
Virtual Reality Applications Center
Iowa State University
Ames, Iowa 50011-2160

Rockwell Collins/Aviation Industries of China
Advanced Technology Symposium Conference
Shanghai, China
May 2000

Abstract
Virtual reality technology provides a unique human-computer interface. This technology can be applied in many situations where individuals need to access or interact with computer data. This paper will present a short overview of the technology and describe current research in developing virtual reality applications focused on engineering design and manufacturing.
